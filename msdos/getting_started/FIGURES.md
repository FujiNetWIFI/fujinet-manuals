# Figures — FujiNet RS-232 MS-DOS Guide to Operations

This edition uses **no photographs**: every illustration is rendered from a
model through `tools/stl2png.py`. This file records where each picture comes
from, and flags the few hardware details that should be **verified against a
real Rev1b unit** before this Guide is considered final.

`make_case.py` assembles the two published `RS232-Rev1b` case shells (Case
Top + Case Bottom) into a closed clamshell, mirrors the single
`Knurled-Knob` STL into the pair that flanks the connector, keeps the
`Button` STL, and **generates** the DB-9 connector (shell + 9 pins) and the
two LED light-pipe domes. The IBM 5150 is wholly parametric (boxes/cylinders)
— a stylized likeness, not a measured model.

## ⚠ Hardware details to verify against a real Rev1b unit

These were inferred from the BOM, schematic, STLs, and firmware pinmap. They
are plausible but **should be confirmed by eye** before final publication:

1. **LED positions and colours.** BOM has `D4 WIFI_LED_WHITE` and
   `D5 BUS_LED_ORANGE`; the Guide places the white WiFi lamp and orange bus
   lamp on the top face near the connector end. Confirm which lamp is which
   and where the light-pipes actually emerge on the case top.
2. **Button identity.** BOM lists `S1`/`S2` (tact) and `S3` (hard reset snap
   dome); firmware `PIN_BUTTON_C` = "Safe reset". The Guide calls one
   "Button A" (firmware update) and one "Reset". Confirm the labels/roles and
   which button is at which edge.
3. **Power.** The Guide states the FujiNet is **USB-C powered** and does *not*
   draw power from the serial port (no power path from the DB-9 in the BOM;
   ESP32-S3 + WiFi needs ~500 mA). Confirm this is the intended/only powering
   method for end users.
4. **microSD orientation** ("contacts up") and **push-push** action — confirm
   against the Rev1b board (the push-push socket is the Rev1b change vs Rev1).
5. **Default serial speed.** The shipping `config.sys` uses
   `FUJI_BPS=115200`; the FUJICOM protocol note cites a firmware power-on
   default of 9600. The Guide presents **115200** as standard. Confirm the
   FujiNet ships expecting 115200 over RS-232 (or adjust the default quoted).
6. **9-pin vs 25-pin.** The board has a **DB-9 female** edge connector that
   mates with a PC's 9-pin male COM port. The Guide tells 25-pin owners to use
   a 9-to-25 adapter. Confirm the connector gender/pinout matches a standard
   PC serial port directly (no null-modem needed).

## CONFIG screens

The screens are **not** images — they are typeset live in `manual.typ` in the
genuine IBM PC ROM font (Px437 IBM CGA), with text taken verbatim from
`fujinet-config/src/msdos/screen.c`. Edit the `crt(...)` blocks in the
Operations section to change them.
